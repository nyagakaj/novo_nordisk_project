GLP-1 Global Demand & Supply Intelligence Platform

A concept project that explores how AI-powered predictive analytics and intuitive dashboards can help pharmaceutical companies like Novo Nordisk anticipate GLP-1 demand, simulate manufacturing scenarios and support more equitable, sustainable access to life-changing therapies.

How this links to the graduate programme

This project illustrates the recent digital business trend of using data platforms and AI forecasting to solve complex supply-chain problems. It shows how I would approach Enterprise IT at Novo Nordisk: combining system thinking, analytics and user- centred design to enable better decisions.

Tech (enabler, not the hero): HTML, Bootstrap, Chart.js, simulated forecasting logic.

Inspired by insights from Novo Nordisk’s 2024 Annual Report.

Digital business trend: AI-powered predictive supply-chain analytics

Across industries, a key shift in digital business is the move from descriptive dashboards to predictive and scenario-based decision platforms. AI-powered forecasting tools are now used to anticipate demand, optimise inventory and stress-test different strategic choices before they are implemented.

In the pharmaceutical industry, this trend is especially relevant for therapies experiencing explosive global demand, such as GLP-1-based medicines for diabetes and obesity. Traditional planning approaches struggle to keep up with rapid uptake, constrained manufacturing capacity and regional access considerations. Digital forecasting platforms can help companies see around corners, making supply chains more resilient, equitable and sustainable.

What this concept demonstrates
  • Understanding of AI forecasting as a digital business trend.
  • Application to GLP-1 capacity and access challenges.
  • System-level view from data to decision-making.
  • Ability to translate strategy into a concrete digital product idea.

Problem in context

  • Rapidly increasing global demand for GLP-1 therapies.
  • Long lead times and finite manufacturing capacity expansions.
  • Risk of regional shortages and inequitable access for vulnerable groups.
  • Need to align commercial, clinical and sustainability objectives.

Conceptual solution

  • A Global GLP-1 Demand & Supply Intelligence Platform that brings together data, forecasting models and visual decision support.
  • AI-inspired forecasting logic to project demand by region and patient segment.
  • Scenario analysis tools to explore capacity changes, market launches and access strategies.
  • A clean web interface where cross-functional teams can explore future states and trade-offs.

System-thinking view: from signal to patient impact

This concept is less about the specific chart and more about how digital tools can support system-level decisions. By connecting forecasting, manufacturing and allocation into a single view, Enterprise IT can help leaders:

  • Anticipate where demand is likely to exceed supply.
  • Test different capacity or launch scenarios before committing resources.
  • Compare strategies that prioritise vulnerable markets or new indications.
  • Integrate sustainability and access metrics into core decision-making.

In my view, this is how the digital business trend of AI-powered forecasting will shape the future of the pharmaceutical industry: not as a standalone “AI feature”, but as an enabler of more transparent, ethical and evidence-driven decisions about how we bring innovative medicines to people who need them.

Future extensions
  • Add a sustainability layer (e.g., CO₂e per scenario, device footprint).
  • Incorporate AI-governance checks for transparency and fairness.
  • Connect to real internal data warehouses via secure APIs.
  • Embed into enterprise planning workflows for cross-functional teams.
